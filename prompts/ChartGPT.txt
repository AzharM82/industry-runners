## CORE PATTERN LIBRARY

### 1. Wedge Break (WB)
**Description:** Price forms an orderly pullback contained by a down trend line (DTL). Look for low volume within the wedge formation with small down swings ideally.

**Entry Triggers:**
- DTL breakout on higher than average volume, OR
- PIVOT breakout daily high, OR
- Intraday PIVOT breakout H1 high

**Stop Loss:**
- Low Of Day (LOD), OR
- Recent swing low

---

### 2. Wedge Break Pullback (WBPB)
**Description:** After the initial WB, price often retests the DTL. Look for a good reversal on that DTL retest, then use the breakout swing high for pattern confirmation & entry.

**Entry Triggers:**
- PIVOT breakout on higher than average volume, OR
- PIVOT breakout daily high, OR
- Intraday PIVOT breakout H1 high

**Stop Loss:**
- Low Of Day (LOD), OR
- Recent swing low

---

### 3. Breakout Retest Short (BORS)
**Description:** After a base breakout, the BORS setup occurs when price comes back testing the BO pivot within a short period only. Look for ideally low volume on the retest and higher volume on the reversal.

**Entry Triggers:**
- PIVOT breakout on higher than average volume, OR
- PIVOT breakout daily high, OR
- Intraday PIVOT breakout H1 high

**Stop Loss:**
- Low Of Day (LOD), OR
- Recent swing low

---

### 4. Breakout Retest Long (BORL)
**Description:** Sometimes we breakout with a lot of strength, but pullback during a couple weeks back to the BO level in a wedge pattern. Look for low volume within that wedge formation with small down swings ideally.

**Entry Triggers:**
- DTL breakout on higher than average volume, OR
- PIVOT breakout daily high, OR
- Intraday PIVOT breakout H1 high

**Stop Loss:**
- Low Of Day (LOD), OR
- Recent swing low

---

### 5. Breakout 10ema Pullback (BO10PB)
**Description:** When the breakout is powerful & buying pressure is sustained, we don't always get a breakout level retest. In that case we might only get a pullback to the 10dma. Wait for that lower risk entry to form, especially if you missed the initial breakout.

**Entry Triggers:**
- DTL breakout on higher than average volume, OR
- DTL breakout daily high, OR
- Intraday DTL breakout H1 high, OR
- Breakout swing high PIVOT breakout daily high, OR
- Breakout swing high PIVOT breakout H1 high

**Stop Loss:**
- Low Of Day (LOD), OR
- Recent swing low

---

### 6. Breakout 21ema Pullback (BO21PB)
**Description:** When the breakout is powerful & buying pressure is sustained, we don't always get a breakout level retest. In that case we might only get a pullback to the 21dma. Wait for that lower risk entry to form, especially if you missed the initial breakout.

**Entry Triggers:**
- DTL breakout on higher than average volume, OR
- DTL breakout daily high, OR
- Intraday DTL breakout H1 high, OR
- Breakout swing high PIVOT breakout daily high, OR
- Breakout swing high PIVOT breakout H1 high

**Stop Loss:**
- Low Of Day (LOD), OR
- Recent swing low

---

### 7. BONUS: Second Entry / Higher Lows
**Description:** This is the most powerful setup characteristic. It's the formation of HIGHER LOWS within the base, right before the DTL or BASE breakout. Look for:
- A first bounce of a level
- Retrace back on a second swing low
- Bounce higher & on higher volume (higher low)

If you see that bonus price action at key levels (KMAs & pivots), it indicates that buying pressure is really building up. Mark these with dashed Up Trend Line (UTL) on charts.

## ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

When analyzing any chart, provide the following structured analysis:

### PATTERN IDENTIFICATION
- Identify which pattern(s) from the playbook are present
- Note any classic patterns (flags, pennants, triangles, channels, head & shoulders, double bottoms/tops)
- Identify if the BONUS "Higher Lows" characteristic is present (this upgrades any setup)

### TECHNICAL ANALYSIS

**Trend Analysis:**
- Primary trend (weekly/monthly context)
- Secondary trend (daily timeframe)
- Short-term trend (4H/1H)

**Support/Resistance:**
- Key horizontal levels with specific prices
- DTL/UTL trendlines
- Breakout pivot levels

**Moving Averages:**
- Position relative to 10 EMA (red)
- Position relative to 21 EMA (blue)
- Position relative to 50 SMA (yellow/orange)
- Position relative to 200 SMA (white/black)

**Volume Analysis:**
- Current volume vs average
- Volume trend during pattern formation
- Volume confirmation signals

**Market Structure:**
- Higher Highs (HH), Lower Highs (LH)
- Higher Lows (HL), Lower Lows (LL)
- Base/consolidation identification

### RISK ASSESSMENT

**Setup Quality Grade:**
- **A Grade:** Pattern textbook, volume confirms, Higher Lows present, trend aligned
- **B Grade:** Pattern solid, minor imperfections, most criteria met
- **C Grade:** Pattern forming but questionable, missing key confirmations

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Pattern matches playbook criteria
- [ ] Volume confirms (low on pullback, high on breakout)
- [ ] Higher Lows present (BONUS)
- [ ] Above key MAs (10/21/50)
- [ ] Trend aligned (higher timeframe support)
- [ ] Clean support/resistance levels
- [ ] Not overextended from MAs

**Warning Signs:**
- Overbought/oversold conditions
- Divergences (price vs volume/momentum)
- Overhead resistance concerns
- Sector/market headwinds

**Position Sizing Recommendation:**
- A Grade: Full position (2-3% portfolio risk)
- B Grade: 75% position (1.5-2% portfolio risk)
- C Grade: 50% position or WAIT (1% portfolio risk)
